Counselling with Me

What to Expect

A confidential, non-judgemental space where you can talk openly about what's on your mind — and begin to find a way forward.

What Is Counselling?

Counselling is a talking therapy between a client and a trained professional. I offer a confidential, non-judgemental space where you can discuss your thoughts, feelings and concerns at your own pace. I listen with compassion and respect, supporting you in making your own decisions — rather than telling you what to do.

Unlike conversations with friends or family, counselling is different because I won't share my own experiences, pass judgement, or prescribe solutions. Instead, we work together to help you identify what's challenging you, understand the triggers behind certain behaviours, and recognise patterns that may be holding you back.

Keeping difficult emotions bottled up can be damaging and leave people feeling stuck and unable to progress. Counselling offers a way through.

How Working Together Can Help

Together, we can work towards positive and lasting change. This might include:

Challenging limiting beliefs and strengthening self-confidence
Identifying and shifting destructive relationship patterns — such as people-pleasing or taking on excessive responsibility
Developing greater self-understanding and reducing inner criticism
Recovering from relational trauma and restoring self-trust
Understanding how childhood experiences affect adult relationships and behaviour

How Long Does It Take?

The length of counselling varies depending on the individual and what you'd like to work on. Some people see meaningful change in as few as six sessions; others find that longer-term work better suits their needs. Progress is reviewed regularly, and you always decide what feels right for you — there's no pressure to continue beyond what you find helpful.

Getting Started

I work with adults and young people aged 14 and over. Before we begin, I offer a free 30-minute telephone consultation — a chance to talk about what's brought you to counselling, ask any questions you might have, and get a sense of whether working together feels like a good fit. There's no obligation to proceed.
